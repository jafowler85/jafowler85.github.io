9/10/17

Do you speak database?

2 weeks down in my Database Administration Fundamentals class. 2 more to go.

Halfway through the class, I feel like I have learned a lot. While we have been getting some experience using the language of SQL, the class is more geared towards databases as whole. Creating them, adding information to tables, and extracting the information that we need.

If becoming a database administrator or developer was my goal, this class would be of more value. But since I am more interested in the data stored within, the lessons on DML (data manipulation language) are more interesting to me. This definitely has me looking forward to my next class, which is SQL Base Programming.

Going back to the emotions I was experiencing prior to the class starting, Anxiousness is no longer there. Excitement is still present, because of what we have been learning so far and what I know is on the horizon in the next class.

Uncertainty is still there, because while I am learning a lot, I want to be able to put the new skills to use. Trying to find ways to do this at work has been difficult. Everything we do in class is through SQL Server and the studio interface, which I do not have access to at work. Having downloaded both SQL Server and SSMS on my home computer, looks like project time at home for the forseeable future.

Changing topics real quick, all of this learning has inspired me to look into other types of coding. I have been working through another site called Codecademy to continue enhancing my web development skills. I am even looking at doing one of their Pro Intensives: a 10 week, bootcamp style course in website building.

So many things to learn and do, and just not enough time in the days to accomplish them all. 2 weeks left, with a "staycation" scheduled for the final week of the class. I'll have more than 1 thing to look forward to in that final week.

8/26/17

Back to School...

Just a few days separate me from returning to a classroom for the first time since April of 2008. Excitement. Anxiousness. Uncertainty. Just some of the thoughts and feelings I’m having right now.

Excitement because of the what and why. After what felt like, quite literally, Googling every data analytics online graduate program I could think of, I realized that there were some technical skills that I lacked before I could really move forward.

After talking to a coworker who was taking a class through a local community college, I thought I would look to see what they had to offer, and there it was. A Data Analytics w/ SQL certificate. 3 classes that looked to cover exactly what I think I need to get started: Database Administration Fundamentals. SQL Base Programming. SQL Advanced Programming.

Anxiousness and Uncertainty because after registering for the first 2 class for the upcoming fall semester, I stumbled upon a website that provided an online curriculum for Data Analytics. Part of this curriculum included courses on SQL. Did I screw myself over by registering and paying for something that I could’ve done online, at my own pace for a much lower cost?

Since signing up for this website (more to come on that) to give some of their free courses a trial, I am more at ease with my decision to take the SQL classes in-person. The benefit of having an in-person instructor that I can ask questions, along with other people to collaborate with, will help me with picking up the material.

Tuesday will be an interesting day. The classes are at the college’s center in the Ballantyne area of Charlotte, while I work in Mooresville. 40 miles apart. Looking to leave work at 4:00 to make sure that I can arrive with time to spare for a class that is from 6:00 to 9:00, 2 nights a week.

Excitement. Anxiousness. Uncertainty. We’ll see which of these thoughts and feelings are still around after Thursday night.
